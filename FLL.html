What is First Lego League?

First Lego League (FLL) is an international robotics competition for children and youth ages 9-16. Teams of young people work together to solve real-world problems using science, technology, engineering, and math (STEM) skills.

Founded in 1998, FLL has grown to include over 100,000 teams from more than 100 countries worldwide. Participants learn valuable life skills while having fun building and programming robots.

FLL Competition Categories

Robot Game

Teams build and program autonomous robots to complete missions on a themed playing field. Points are scored by completing challenges and navigating obstacles.

Innovation Project

Teams identify a real-world problem in their community and develop innovative solutions. They create prototypes and present their findings to judges.

Core Values

FLL emphasizes important life skills like teamwork, respect, and inclusion. Teams are evaluated on how well they demonstrate these values throughout the season.

Teamwork

Participants learn collaboration skills by working in diverse teams. Each member contributes unique talents to achieve common goals.

STEM Learning

FLL introduces participants to Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math concepts through hands-on activities and problem-solving challenges.

Competition

Teams compete at local tournaments and can advance to regional, national, and international championships, showcasing their skills and innovations.

The Impact of FLL

Skills Development

  • Critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Teamwork and collaboration
  • Programming and engineering skills
  • Public speaking and presentation
  • Research and innovation

Career Inspiration

FLL participants are more likely to pursue STEM careers and higher education. Many alumni become engineers, scientists, and entrepreneurs, contributing to technological advancement and innovation.
